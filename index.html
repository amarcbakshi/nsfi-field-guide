◊ The team you inherited

Most teams are running forms no one designed.

The standup. The all-hands. The retrospective. The roadmap review. The 1:1. These forms were borrowed, adapted, or defaulted into—rarely built from first principles, almost never redesigned once they're in place. They move information. They don't necessarily build trust, coordination, or collective intelligence.

This manifests as a management problem. At root, it is a design problem.

A small number of practitioners—scattered across industries, vocabularies, and disciplines—treat teams as designed artifacts. They ask what structure produces what kind of conversation, what role configurations change how decisions get made, what happens when you redesign how a team reflects, decides, or disagrees. They often work in isolation from one another without a shared vocabulary and research base.

New Social Forms is building that foundation—starting with teams.

◉ Year One Focus

Teams.

Teams are under more structural pressure than they've faced in decades. We're treating that as a design problem.

AI is changing who does what—and by extension, what a team is for. Remote and hybrid norms have disrupted the spatial forms that teams relied on without anyone naming them as forms. The coordination patterns that made sense for one era are running on inertia into another.

Most organizations are responding by adding tools or adjusting processes. Fewer are asking the structural question: what kind of team form does this moment actually require?

That is our question.

↳ Where this goes

Year One begins with teams. Over multiple years, nSFi will move across collective decision-making, care and healing, civic participation, learning, and conflict and repair. Social forms are vital to our mental, social, and political lives. But teams come first.

‡ Workstreams

We're launching four workstreams,
all pointed at teams in Year One.

I

Gatherings

Salons, field trips, research retreats, workshops, and offsites — bringing practitioners, researchers, technologists, and team designers together to exchange insights, organize, and surface what the field doesn't yet have language for.

II

Community Knowledge Hub

A living, AI-assisted, human-reviewed hub holding practitioner profiles, structured 1:1 interviews, small-group conversation briefs, and the initiative's public-facing storytelling. AI-generated content is flagged until a human has reviewed it — non-negotiable.

III

Field Pilots & Prototyping

We partner with teams open to experimentation, introduce new social forms or CCC prototypes, and study what happens. Modest, learning-oriented experiments. Each one produces an evaluation brief. Not a product. A research partnership.

IV

Research

Four directions on teams: a taxonomy of team forms, simulating structural variation with LLMs, workplace diagnostics, and an evaluation framework that travels across team contexts.

✦ Research

Four directions we're exploring,
applied to teams.

01 · Taxonomy

Taxonomy of team forms

Build a shared vocabulary for the structural components of team forms: input boundaries, process architecture, role structure, epistemic inputs, output mechanisms, feedback and accountability, spatial and material conditions, hierarchy and power.

02 · Build & simulate

Build and simulate

Use LLMs and computational tools to model what changes when a team swaps one structural element for another, generate hypotheses, and check predictions against practitioner intuition and real-world experiments. A structured validation protocol—generating novel structural combinations, simulating dynamics, presenting to practitioners for blind evaluation.

03 · Diagnostics

Workplace design and diagnostics

Develop a diagnostic that helps teams map their existing forms and surface the structural tensions no one has named. Workplace pilot partnerships allow sustained collaboration over months—introducing new forms from the research and measuring what changes.

04 · Evaluation

Evaluation framework

Design, prototype, and test an evaluation framework that works across team settings: structural (did the form do what it was designed to do), experiential (what was it like to be in the room), and relational (what changed in the relationships over time).

⌂ About · Motivation

Teams are the social form most people live inside. They deserve to be designed.

Most of us spend our working lives inside teams someone else inherited. The structures that shape how we think, decide, and produce together are rarely chosen — and almost never redesigned once they're in place.

We started New Social Forms because the pressure on teams is real and rising — from AI, from hybrid work, from rates of change the inherited forms were never built for. And the people who actually design new team forms — facilitators, organizational designers, researchers, technologists — work without a shared vocabulary, without a shared research base, and often without recognizing what they do as design.

We want to change that.

The MIT Center for Constructive Communication brings deep expertise in how people communicate and make meaning together — a builder culture that prototypes and tests in real-world settings, and research infrastructure that moves methods from the lab into the field. Google Xi brings reach into the conditions teams actually face inside organizations.

Year One focuses on teams. Over time, nSFi will expand: collective decision-making, civic participation, care and healing, learning, conflict and repair. But teams come first because teams are where the most people will feel the change.

? FAQ

Questions we hear,
answered plainly.

What is New Social Forms?+

A multi-year research and practice initiative at the MIT Center for Constructive Communication, launched in cooperation with Google Xi. Year One focuses on teams: the most universal social form, and the one facing the most immediate structural pressure.

Why teams?+

Teams are the social form most people work inside. They are also the form under the most acute pressure right now—from AI, hybrid work, and shifting expectations of what work itself is for. And they are designable. Most aren't being designed; they're being inherited. We want to change that.

What does "social form" mean?+

A social form is the structure inside which people do something together. A standup is a form. A citizen assembly is a form. A retro, a town hall, a 1:1, a war room, a roadmap review—all forms. The form determines what kinds of conversation, decision, and coordination are possible.

What are you actually producing in Year One?+

A taxonomy of team forms. An annotated practitioner library. A diagnostic tool for workplaces. Evaluation briefs from field pilots. A corpus of structured 1:1s with practitioners. Computational simulations of structural variation. A public knowledge hub. Plus salons, gatherings, and a small Prototyping Fund.

Is this consulting?+

No. nSFi is a research initiative. Field pilots are research partnerships, not consulting engagements. We don't sell services. Partners contribute a willing team and time; we contribute a prototype, a research frame, and an evaluation brief.

What does Year One look like, month to month?+

Year One stands up the four workstreams—gatherings, the community knowledge hub, field pilots, and research—and runs 12–15 structured conversations with practitioners. We launch a Prototyping Fund in July. Field pilots run summer through fall. Initial outputs publish in late fall.

Who's behind it?+

Dimitra Dimitrakopoulou is Principal Investigator. Amar Bakshi leads the community knowledge hub. Kari Mulholland leads gatherings. Maya Detwiller leads field pilots. The initiative sits inside MIT CCC, with Deb Roy as the academic anchor, in partnership with Google Xi.

How is AI involved?+

Two ways. First, the substrate: AI is changing what teams need to be able to do, and that's part of why we're doing this work now. Second, the method: we use LLMs and computational tools to model what changes when a team swaps one structural element for another, and we use AI assistance in the knowledge hub—always flagged until a human reviews.

Why MIT and Google Xi?+

MIT CCC has a rare combination of communication research, builder culture, and field-grade infrastructure for working with real institutions. Google Xi brings reach into the conditions teams face inside organizations, and an interest in what better team forms could look like at scale. Each contributes something the other doesn't have.

Will the work eventually go beyond teams?+

Yes. Year One starts with teams because teams are where the most people will feel the change. Over multiple years nSFi will expand into other expressions of social form: collective decision-making, civic participation, care and healing, learning, conflict and repair.

How do I get involved?+

Join the practitioner community. Apply your team for a field pilot. Come to a salon. Submit to the Prototyping Fund (open call, July). If none of those fit, reach out anyway.
