01
◊ The team you inherited

Most teams are running forms no one designed.

The standup. The all-hands. The retrospective. The roadmap review. The 1:1. These forms were borrowed, adapted, or defaulted into—rarely built from first principles, almost never redesigned once they're in place. They move information. They don't necessarily build trust, coordination, or collective intelligence.

This isn't a management problem. It's a design problem.

Form A · Inheritedstandup, round-robin
Form B · Redesignedsilent + LLM-augmented

A small number of practitioners—scattered across industries, vocabularies, and disciplines—treat teams as designed artifacts. They mostly work in isolation. There is no shared vocabulary, no shared research base. nSFi is building that foundation, starting with teams.

02
◉ Year One · Focus

Teams are under more structural pressure than they've faced in decades.

AI is changing who does what—and by extension, what a team is for. Remote and hybrid norms have disrupted the spatial forms that teams relied on without anyone naming them as forms. The coordination patterns that made sense for one era are running on inertia into another.

Most organizations are responding by adding tools or adjusting processes. Fewer are asking the structural question: what kind of team form does this moment actually require? That is nSFi's question.

‡ Workstreams

Four workstreams. All pointed at teams.

In Year One, every nSFi workstream takes teams as its subject.

Gatherings

nSFi brings practitioners, researchers, technologists, and team designers together—in salons, field trips, research retreats, workshops, and offsites—to exchange insights, test prototypes, and surface what the field doesn't yet have language for.

I

Community Knowledge Hub

A living, AI-assisted, human-reviewed hub for practitioner profiles, structured 1:1 interviews, small-group conversation briefs, and nSFi's public-facing storytelling. AI-generated content is flagged until a human has reviewed it—non-negotiable.

II

Field Pilots & Prototyping

nSFi partners with teams open to experimentation, introduces new social forms or CCC prototypes, and studies what happens. Modest, learning-oriented experiments. Each one produces an evaluation brief. Not a product. A research partnership.

III

Research

Four research directions on teams: a taxonomy of team forms, simulating structural variation with LLMs, workplace diagnostics, and an evaluation framework that travels across team contexts.

IV
04
✦ Research

Four directions, applied to teams.

Year One initiates each. None is expected to be exhausted in Year One.

01 · Taxonomy

Taxonomy of team forms

A shared vocabulary for the structural components of team forms: input boundaries, process architecture, role structure, epistemic inputs, output mechanisms, feedback and accountability, spatial and material conditions, hierarchy and power. Anchored on forms CCC knows well; refined through the practitioner library.

02 · Build & simulate

Build and simulate

LLMs and computational tools model what changes when a team swaps one structural element for another. The validation protocol—generate, simulate, blind-evaluate with practitioners—is itself a publishable contribution.

03 · Diagnostics

Workplace design and diagnostics

A diagnostic that helps teams map their existing forms and surface the structural tensions no one has named. Workplace pilots allow sustained collaboration over months, introducing new forms and measuring what changes.

04 · Evaluation

Evaluation framework

An evaluation framework that travels across team settings: structural (did the form do what it was designed to do), experiential (what was it like to be in the room), and relational (what changed in the relationships over time).

06
@ About · Motivation

Why we do this.

Teams are the social form most people live inside. They deserve to be designed.

Most of us spend our working lives inside teams someone else inherited. The structures that shape how we think, decide, and produce together are rarely chosen—and almost never redesigned once they're in place.

We started nSFi · New Social Forms because that gap matters. The pressure on teams is real and rising—from AI, from hybrid work, from rates of change the inherited forms were never built for. And the people who actually design new team forms—facilitators, organizational designers, researchers, technologists—work without a shared vocabulary, without a shared research base, and often without recognizing what they do as design.

We want to change that.

The MIT Center for Constructive Communication brings deep expertise in how people communicate and make meaning together—a builder culture that prototypes and tests in real-world settings, and research infrastructure that moves methods from the lab into the field. Google Xi brings reach into the conditions teams actually face inside organizations.

Year One focuses on teams. Over time, nSFi will expand: collective decision-making, civic participation, care and healing, learning, conflict and repair. But teams come first because teams are where the most people will feel the change.

? FAQ

Questions we hear, answered plainly.

Q01What is nSFi?+

nSFi (New Social Forms) is a multi-year research and practice initiative at the MIT Center for Constructive Communication, launched in cooperation with Google Xi. Year One focuses on teams: the most universal social form, and the one facing the most immediate structural pressure.

Q02Why teams?+

Teams are the social form most people work inside. They are also the form under the most acute pressure right now—from AI, hybrid work, and shifting expectations of what work itself is for. And they are designable. Most aren't being designed; they're being inherited. nSFi wants to change that.

Q03What does "social form" mean?+

A social form is the structure inside which people do something together. A standup is a form. A citizen assembly is a form. A retro, a town hall, a 1:1, a war room, a roadmap review—all forms. The form determines what kinds of conversation, decision, and coordination are possible.

Q04What is nSFi actually producing in Year One?+

A taxonomy of team forms. An annotated practitioner library. A diagnostic tool for workplaces. Evaluation briefs from field pilots. A corpus of structured 1:1s with practitioners. Computational simulations of structural variation. A public knowledge hub. Plus salons, gatherings, and a small Prototyping Fund.

Q05Is this consulting?+

No. nSFi is a research initiative. Field pilots are research partnerships, not consulting engagements. We don't sell services. Partners contribute a willing team and time; nSFi contributes a prototype, a research frame, and an evaluation brief.

Q06How is AI involved?+

Two ways. First, the substrate: AI is changing what teams need to be able to do, and that's part of why nSFi exists now. Second, the method: we use LLMs and computational tools to model what changes when a team swaps one structural element for another, and we use AI assistance in the knowledge hub—always flagged until a human reviews.

Q07Why MIT and Google Xi?+

MIT CCC has a rare combination of communication research, builder culture, and field-grade infrastructure for working with real institutions. Google Xi (Experience Institute) brings reach into the conditions teams face inside organizations, and an interest in what better team forms could look like at scale.

Q08Will nSFi eventually go beyond teams?+

Yes. Year One starts with teams because teams are where the most people will feel the change. Over multiple years nSFi will expand into other expressions of social form: collective decision-making, civic participation, care and healing, learning, conflict and repair.

Q09How do I get involved?+

Join the practitioner community. Apply your team for a field pilot. Come to a salon. Submit to the Prototyping Fund (open call, July). If none of those fit, reach out anyway.
